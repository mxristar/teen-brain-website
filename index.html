Introduction

Social media is a huge part of teen life today. Apps like TikTok, Instagram, and Snapchat are fun, but they also affect how the brain works. This webpage shows how social media changes the teenage brain, using research and brain science to explain what’s going on behind the screen.

What Happens in the Brain

When teens use social media, the brain releases dopamine, the “feel good” chemical. This happens when we get likes, comments, or new followers. The reward system in the brain lights up—kind of like how it does with food or candy. Teens’ brains are still developing, so they’re more sensitive to this kind of reward, making social media extra addicting.

Brain Connection

  • Prefrontal cortex: decision-making and impulse control is still developing in teens.
  • Amygdala: handles emotions and becomes more active with online reactions.

The Downsides

  • Too much social media can lead to anxiety, depression, and low self-esteem.
  • It can mess with sleep, since many teens stay up late scrolling.
  • It makes it harder to focus in school because the brain gets used to short, fast content.
