Introduction

Social media has rapidly become a central part of teenage life. While it allows teens to connect, share, and learn, it also has deep neurological and psychological effects. This project explores how social media use impacts the adolescent brain, focusing on reward processing, self-esteem, identity development, and cognitive functioning. As teens are especially sensitive to peer approval and novelty, platforms like TikTok and Instagram often take advantage of these brain traits, leading to both positive reinforcement and negative psychological patterns.
